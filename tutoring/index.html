03 — Teach & Mentor

Tutoring

I believe strong academic foundations help students feel capable, confident, and prepared for the future. My tutoring approach focuses not only on improving grades, but also on building habits, confidence, and independence that students can carry with them long-term.

I’m especially passionate about two foundational skills: daily reading and quick math fluency. Research consistently shows that students who read regularly — even just 20 minutes a day — build stronger vocabulary, comprehension, focus, and academic confidence. In the same way, mastering quick math facts gives students the mental flexibility they need for problem-solving, higher-level math, and future success.

Most importantly, I love working with children and helping them realize that they are capable of more than they think. Every child deserves to feel successful, supported, and confident in their learning — because they are the future.

Approach

About my approach

My tutoring philosophy is rooted in patience, encouragement, and consistency. I work with each student as an individual, identifying where they need support while also helping them build the confidence to take ownership of their learning.

I strongly believe that small daily habits create major academic growth. Reading every day, practicing math facts, staying organized, and learning how to study effectively can make a lasting difference in a student’s confidence and performance. My goal is to help students strengthen these habits in a way that feels manageable, encouraging, and sustainable.

Philosophy

My tutoring philosophy

I believe tutoring should be encouraging, structured, and personalized. Every student learns differently, so I adapt my approach based on each child’s personality, learning style, and academic goals.

My goal is to make learning feel less intimidating and more achievable. Whether a student needs help catching up, staying ahead, preparing for a test, or building stronger study habits, I focus on creating a supportive environment where they feel comfortable asking questions and trying again.

Foundations

Why foundational skills matter

Strong foundations in reading and math can shape a student’s entire academic experience. Daily reading helps students become stronger thinkers, writers, and communicators. Quick math fluency helps students solve problems faster and with more confidence.

When students build these skills early, they are better prepared for future classes, standardized tests, and lifelong learning.

Common questions

What ages do you tutor?

I work with students from 1st through 12th grade, adapting pacing and expectations to each child’s grade level and goals.

What subjects do you tutor?

Coding, math, English, Spanish, SAT prep, and study skills & time management. The subject cards above outline the focus of each — reach out if you want to talk through what fits your child.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes. I offer both in-person tutoring in Preston Hollow / Highland Park and virtual sessions.

Many families find weekly sessions create the steadiest progress. We can also adjust frequency around tests, busy seasons, or specific goals.

Do you help with homework and test prep?

Yes — sessions can include homework support, quiz and test review, and longer-term prep for finals or the SAT.
